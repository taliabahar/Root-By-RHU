Traveling between destinations in Bonn is not only easy, but convenient, as their public transportation network is well-developed with city sites and residential neighborhoods within close proximity. For those without their own cars, Bonn has excellent public transportations including, but not limited to buses, trams, subways, city trains, and several Rhine Ferries.

Bus and Tram Information

Bus and tram stops are marked by a sign with a green H in a green circle against a yellow background. The number of buses/trams that stop there are usually listen there together with their the frequency of their arrival/departure.

Bus and Tram Ticket Information

Tickets may be purchased from vending machines on trams, underground trains, and various ticket offices in Bonn. On buses, tickets may also be purchased from the driver. Train Information

Many European capitals and major cities are connected via train. Brussels is three hours away, Paris four hours, and Berlin four and a half hours from Bonn's main railway station. Car Sharing

Need a city car or van every now and again? For a car pooling arrangement, you can register with several companies and book your car by phone. There is a basic fee for your registration and the administration of clients, as well as a travel fee for the car you are planning to use. For more information, visit: Car Sharing
