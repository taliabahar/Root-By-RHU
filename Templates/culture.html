Food to Try
  • Black Forest Cake

    • chocolate sponge cake sandwiched with whipped cream and cherries

  • Bratwurst

    • a sausage usually made of beef, pork, or veal

  • Breaded Pork Schnitzel

    • meat, usually thinned by pounding with a meat tenderizer, that is fried

  • Königsberger Klopse

    • this dish consists of veal meatballs in a creamy white sauce made with capers and lemon juice; traditionally served with boiled potatoes

  • Sauerbraten

    • pot roast that can be prepared with a variety of meats

  • Spätzle Noodles

    • Traditional Swabian Egg Noodles

  • Stollen

    • a bread made of dried or candied fruit, marzipan, nuts like almonds, and spices like cinnamon and cardamom

  • Strudel

    • layered pastry with a filling that is usually sweet

Cultural Etiquette

Table Manners

Keep your knife in your left hand and your fork in your right hand. Don't switch.

Hands should stay visible above the table.

Keep elbows off of the table.

When finished, lay fork and knife across the right side of the plate

Tipping

Guide: 5-10% per day

Waitstaff: 10-15%

**Hand your tip directly to your server.

Bartender: 10%

Taxi Drivers: round up to the nearest €

Miscellaneous

Be on time. Punctuality is highly valued in Germany.

Keep your hands out of your pockets when you talk to someone.

Avoid shouting or chewing gum in public.
